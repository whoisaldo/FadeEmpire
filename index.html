Chapter V

The Barbers.

Two chairs, two sets of hands. Pick your barber, keep your barber.

Hassan,
Master Barber.

Hassan trained in Lebanon before opening Fade Empire in 2018. Seven years and two thousand cuts in, he still uses the same pair of scissors he started with — sharpened twice a year, never replaced.

His house style is restraint: a fade that earns its name without screaming, a beard that looks like you grew it that way, edges that hold for two weeks. He works on one head at a time.

"A good haircut is one that nobody compliments — they just look at you longer."

In the shop 10 AM – 6 PM · Off Tuesdays

Javier,
The Second Chair.

Javier holds down the second chair six days a week. Clean tapers, sharp lineups, classic scissor work — and the first hot towel of the day goes on at nine sharp.

Same standard, different hands. If Hassan's book is full — or Tuesday is your only day — Javier has the chair ready from 9 AM, Monday through Saturday.

In the shop 9 AM – 6 PM · Mon–Sat
